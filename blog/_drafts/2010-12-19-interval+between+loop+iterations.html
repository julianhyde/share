The loop is simple: all it does is compute the number of milliseconds since the last iteration of the loop, and store the result in a histogram. The goal was not to measure the performance of the loop, but to look at the variations in the intervals between loop iterations. Those variations would presumably be caused by the Linux scheduler.

The vast majority of iterations occur 0 milliseconds after the previous iteration. No surprise there; Java's clock granularity, 1 millisecond, is coarse enough to contain a few million clock cycles.
